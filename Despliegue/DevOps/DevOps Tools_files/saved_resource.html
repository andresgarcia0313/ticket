                  Dan Gordon Kostis 6 months ago edited

                  Thanks for sharing your input Kostis!

                  Let me start by saying that we are in no way trying to be sneaky, dishonest, or deceitful with this data or how it is presented, but we dont always get things right, or completed all at once (we operate by MVC). Having said that, Ill respond to each point individually:

                  RE: GitLab/CodeFresh comparison is inaccurate:

                  We are striving to have as accurate information about all tools as we can, as we believe that accurate information benefits everyone in the long run. However, with so many tools, the frequency of changes in them (especially nowadays with CD!) and so much data to collect and present we dont always get it all correct (although we try our hardest to).

                  This is where the GitLab mission of everyone can contribute and our value of collaboration come into play. If there are ever corrections to make, or data to add, please feel free to contribute it. You can either edit the page/info yourself and send us a merge request, open an issue and well look into it, or email the necessary changes to devopstools@gitlab.com. (all this info is also listed at the bottom of each tool page). Commenting on the page as you have is a good start too. Ive opened this Issue to track adding CodeFresh to the Package stage.

                  In short, thanks for letting us know when we missed the mark, and please help us get it right.

                  RE: GitLab in same column as TerraForm, Ansible, etc

                  Thank you, youve made us realize that the devops-tools page is not being clear on what it is presenting. We are building out this page to be a resource (eventually) representing the whole DevOps toolchain landscape. What is there is what weve been able to put in so far. Weve broken the toolsets into what we see as the stages of the DevOps life cycle (adapted from the DevOps wikipedia page). And while GitLab does not have most of the same functionality as TerraForm, Ansible, Chef, Puppet, etc, which are in the Configure stage, it does have capabilities that we do classify as Configure such as helping with Kubernetes configuration and secret configuration variable management. You can find more details on what capabilities GitLab has in each stage & category by clicking on the GitLab icon at the top of each stage.

                  Similarly for Heroku and Snyk.

                  Ive opened a GitLab Issue so we can track making the intent of this page more clear.

                  Finally, RE: your CodeFresh comparison:

                  It is a very nice piece of work with lots of great data (I've linked it above so others can find it). Thank you for writing such a great piece. We have previously linked to it from our CodeFresh tool page, and identified where you are correct about what GitLab doesnt have yet, in addition to our Issues tracking each item. This is a great example of how us vendors can all keep pushing each other to become better for the industry and the community we are all a part of.

                  Thanks for helping to keep us honest, and we hope everyone will find value in this resource we are building (hopefully with your's and everyones help).
